    VNUHCM and Finland University to cooperate in training Bachelor of IT
    14 Mar,2018

    VNUHCM and Finland University to cooperate in training Bachelor of IT

    On March 8th, at VNUHCM - University of Science, Department of Science and Technology of HCMC and Finland University held an educational seminar entitled "The role of information technology in constructing a smartcity" . Attending the conference was Associate Prof. Vu Hai Quan, VNUHCM's Vice President and Mr. Kari Kahilouto, Ambassador of Finland in Vietnam.

    There were 6 seminars by experts from the University of Turku, Finland and VNUHCM on smartcity model, technologies to develop a smartcity including: Internet of Things (IoT), cloud computing, Big Data, etc.

    In reference to smartcity 2030: The trends and patterns of Dr. Sari Puustinen, University of Turku are considered as highlights of the program as they present the big picture of the correlation of the 6 principal elements in a smartcity development cycle. Knowledge potential and IT human capital in the 4.0 industrial revolution are strongly stressed.

    In addition, the importance of ensuring the network security and the information infrastructure when building the model were also discussed. These are the perspectives on the urgency and development potential of national network security management projects in the upcoming years, heading towards the realization of future smartcities in Vietnam.

    On this occasion, VNUHCM and Finland University also signed a contract on training cooperation of Bachelor of Network Security with 2 + 1 model.

    Representatives from VNUHCM and Finland University on the MoU signing ceremony

    Students will study for 2 years in VNUHCM, then they will transfer to University of Turku, Finland to complete the program in the last year. Additionally, students in Vietnam also have the opportunity to take short-term courses at one of the 4 major universities in Finland through the Summer Camp program co-organized by VNUHCM and the University of Finland. Both programs are expected to receive enrollment applications from 2018 April.
