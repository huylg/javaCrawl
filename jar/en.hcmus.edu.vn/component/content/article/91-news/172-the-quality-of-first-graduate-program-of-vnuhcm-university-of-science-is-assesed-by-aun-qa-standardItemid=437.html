    The first graduate program of VNUHCM-University of Science is assessed by AUN-QA standard

    On October 10th – 12th, 2018, Biotechnology graduate program of VNUHCM-University of Science Science was assessed by AUN-QA standard.

    Two representative members of AUN-QA standard assessment team are Mr. Havalit Wongse-ek (Mahidol University, Thailand) and Mr. Irwan Trinugroho (Sebelas Maret University, Indonesia) came to work at VNUHCM-University of Science.  

    Assessment team with Dean/Associate Dean, Education and Research Council representatives and Self-assessment report authors (from Faculty of Biology-Biotechnology)

    During the first 2 working days, the assessment team interviewed and visited the related parties of the training program. On October 12th, 2018, the assessment team summarized the good parts and provide some recommendations for improvement for the Biotechnology Graduate Program.

     

    The assessment team and the representatives of recruitment 

    Overall, the AUN-QA standard assessment Program in the Biotechnology graduate program has been a success. Representatives of the Faculty of Biology - Biotechnology sent their thanks to teachers, employers, departments, students and alumni who enthusiastically supported the evaluation.

    The Faculty of Biology and Biotechnology (FBB) (it was named as Faculty of Biology before 01/05/2015) is one of the faculties that has been rooted since the early day of the HCMUS. Currently, FBB has 8 departments (Genetics, Biochemistry, Microbiology, Plant Physiology, Animal Physiology and Biotechnology, Ecology and Evolutional Biology, Molecular & Environmental Biotechnology and Plant Biotechnology & Biotransformation), 13 laboratories, 1 center, and 3 cooperated laboratories which are in charge of the academic and research activities. We provide two undergraduate programs which are Biology and Biotechnology program with more than 500 enrolling students and supply about 400 graduates to the workforce each year. Our programs aim to provide students with a modern education in biology and biotechnology so that they are likely to continue into graduate programs, teaching profession or other careers in agricultural, chemical, clinical, political, policy and industrial sectors of society.
